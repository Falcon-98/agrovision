Project Scope

Literature Survey

Traditional agricultural practices in Sri Lanka face significant challenges, particularly affecting smallholder farmers who cultivate crops such as paddy, vegetables, and fruits across diverse agro-climatic zones. Conventional methods of pest and disease detection rely heavily on manual scouting, which is labor-intensive, time-consuming, and prone to human error. Arnal Barbedo (2013) highlighted the limitations of manual techniques, noting their subjectivity and environmental variability, which often result in delayed or inaccurate diagnoses.


Soil health monitoring has similarly evolved from traditional laboratory-based analysis to real-time, sensor-driven systems. Manual soil sampling, while accurate, is slow and impractical for continuous monitoring, often leading to delayed interventions that affect crop productivity. Jones (2004) noted that delays in identifying nutrient deficiencies can result in suboptimal fertilization and nutrient leaching, exacerbating environmental degradation.


Water management is also vital for sustainable farming. Traditional irrigation systems often waste water because they follow fixed schedules and don't consider real-time changes in weather, soil moisture, or crop growth. Agriculture accounts for nearly 70% of freshwater usage worldwide, yet traditional irrigation methods are notoriously inefficient, leading to significant water loss through evaporation, runoff, and over-irrigation.


Recent advancements in AI have transformed these agricultural domains. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have dramatically improved image-based classification systems in agriculture. Brahimi et al. (2017) demonstrated the effectiveness of CNNs in classifying tomato plant diseases, achieving over 95% accuracy using a large annotated dataset. However, such supervised learning approaches require extensive labeled data, which is often scarce in agricultural contexts.


The integration of IoT-based sensors has enabled automated data collection for parameters such as soil moisture, pH, and electrical conductivity (EC). Platforms like Teralytic and Microsoft FarmBeats have deployed distributed sensor networks to monitor soil conditions, but these systems often require stable internet connectivity and expensive hardware, making them less viable for small-scale farmers in remote areas.


Key Research Findings:

  • Self-supervised learning techniques show promise in reducing dependency on labeled agricultural data
  • Edge computing enables real-time processing without cloud dependency
  • Ensemble learning methods (Random Forest, XGBoost) effectively handle noisy agricultural datasets
  • LSTM networks excel at forecasting soil moisture and crop water requirements
  • Reinforcement learning can optimize irrigation policies while balancing water conservation and crop health

Research Gap

Lack of Integrated Systems: Most existing solutions focus on individual components, requiring farmers to adopt multiple tools, which increases complexity and cost. There is no seamless integration between pest detection, soil monitoring, and irrigation management functionalities.

Cloud Dependency: Many precision agriculture systems rely heavily on cloud infrastructure for data processing and analytics, which poses significant barriers in rural areas with limited internet connectivity, introducing latency and dependency on network availability.

High Costs: Commercial platforms often require expensive hardware, distributed sensor networks, and operate on subscription-based models that are financially burdensome for low-income smallholder farmers in developing regions.

Limited Labeled Data: Most deep learning models require extensive manual annotation to perform well, which is expensive and time-consuming to produce in diverse agricultural contexts, particularly for varied pest and disease types.

Class Imbalance Issues: Many datasets underrepresent less frequent pest and disease types, leading to biased models that perform poorly on minority classes, which can be critical for early intervention.

Limited Edge AI Application: The application of advanced AI techniques like self-supervised learning, reinforcement learning, and edge computing remains underexplored in agricultural contexts, particularly for resource-limited environments.

Research Problem & Solution

The Challenge

Smallholder farmers in Sri Lanka face multiple interconnected agricultural challenges.

Agriculture in Sri Lanka employs over 25% of the workforce and contributes approximately 7% to the national GDP. However, smallholder farmers often lack access to advanced technologies, making them vulnerable to crop losses and resource inefficiencies. Traditional farming methods lead to:

  • Delayed pest and disease interventions
  • Inefficient fertilizer application
  • Water wastage and poor irrigation scheduling
  • Reduced crop yields and economic losses

Our Solution: Integrated AI System

A comprehensive edge-deployed platform combining three critical agricultural components.

Our AI-enhanced precision agriculture system integrates pest detection, soil health monitoring, and irrigation optimization into a single, edge-deployed platform. Key innovations include:

  • Self-supervised learning for pest detection with minimal labeled data
  • LoRa IoT network for reliable rural connectivity
  • Hybrid AI models for accurate soil nutrient prediction
  • LSTM-DQN-MPC framework for smart irrigation
  • NVIDIA Jetson Nano for real-time edge processing

Expected Impact

By providing reliable, scalable, and sustainable agricultural monitoring, our system aims to:

  1. Reduce pesticide use by up to 38% through targeted interventions
  2. Achieve 91.3% accuracy in nutrient deficiency predictions
  3. Reduce water usage by 32% while improving yields by 12%
  4. Enable real-time decision-making with 110-second end-to-end latency
  5. Provide affordable solutions at LKR 12,000 per soil monitoring unit

Research Objectives

Drone-Based Pest Detection

Develop a self-supervised ResNet-50 model using drone imagery to detect pest and disease outbreaks with 91.2% accuracy and minimal labeled data dependency.

Real-Time Soil Monitoring

Create a LoRa IoT network with hybrid CNN-XGBoost models to provide continuous soil health insights and nutrient recommendations with 91.3% accuracy.

Smart Irrigation Control

Implement LSTM forecasting, DQN reinforcement learning, and MPC for adaptive irrigation scheduling, achieving 32% water savings and 12% yield improvement.

Edge Computing Integration

Deploy all AI models on NVIDIA Jetson Nano for real-time processing with under 110-second latency, ensuring connectivity-independent operation.

Farmer-Friendly Interface

Develop unified web and mobile dashboards using Flutter and Flask, providing intuitive access to pest alerts, soil metrics, and irrigation schedules.

Field Validation

Conduct extensive field trials across diverse Sri Lankan agricultural sites to validate system performance, usability, and impact on farming practices.

Ultimate Goal

Create an affordable, scalable, and sustainable precision agriculture platform that empowers smallholder farmers in Sri Lanka with real-time, data-driven insights to improve crop productivity, reduce resource waste, and promote environmentally responsible farming practices.

Methodology

Our AI-enhanced precision agriculture system employs a comprehensive methodology integrating three core subsystems into a cohesive edge-deployed platform. The system operates on an NVIDIA Jetson Nano, ensuring real-time performance and autonomy in low-connectivity environments.

System Architecture

Comprehensive system architecture showing the integration of drone-based pest detection, LoRa soil monitoring, and smart irrigation systems

Key Components & Features

Aerial Pest Monitoring

DJI Phantom 4 drone with RGB/multispectral cameras for automated field surveillance and pest detection using ResNet-50 with focal loss.

LoRa IoT Network

Long-range, low-power communication for soil sensors, achieving 98.7% transmission success rate over 4km range in rural areas.

Hybrid AI Models

Self-supervised learning for pest detection, CNN-XGBoost for soil analysis, and LSTM-DQN-MPC for irrigation optimization.

Edge Processing

NVIDIA Jetson Nano enables real-time inference with 1.3s per image for pest detection and 1.6s per sample for soil analysis.

Sustainable Power

Solar panels with lithium-ion battery backup ensure 96.8% uptime for continuous 24/7 operation in remote locations.

Unified Dashboard

Flutter mobile app and Flask web interface provide farmers with integrated access to all system outputs and recommendations.

Data Flow Process

  1. Data Acquisition: Drone captures field imagery, soil sensors collect nutrient data, environmental sensors monitor irrigation parameters.
  2. Data Transmission: Images transferred via Wi-Fi, soil data via LoRa network, irrigation data via ESP32 to Jetson Nano.
  3. AI Processing: Edge inference using optimized models - ResNet-50 for pests, CNN-XGBoost for soil, LSTM-DQN-MPC for irrigation.
  4. Decision Making: System generates pest alerts, nutrient recommendations, and irrigation schedules based on AI outputs.
  5. User Interface: Unified dashboard displays results as heatmaps, metrics, and actionable recommendations.
  6. Feedback Loop: Farmer actions and system performance data used to continuously improve model accuracy.

Technologies Used

Our integrated precision agriculture system leverages cutting-edge technologies across hardware, software, and AI domains:

TensorFlow & PyTorch

Deep learning frameworks for implementing ResNet-50, CNN-XGBoost, and LSTM models with TensorRT optimization for edge deployment.

Flutter & Flask

Cross-platform mobile development and web framework for creating unified farmer interfaces with real-time data visualization.

NVIDIA Jetson Nano

Edge computing platform for real-time AI inference with GPU acceleration, enabling autonomous operation in low-connectivity areas.

LoRaWAN

Long-range, low-power wireless communication protocol for reliable data transmission from soil sensors over 4km range.

DJI Phantom 4

Professional drone platform with RGB and multispectral cameras for automated aerial surveillance and pest detection imaging.

SQLite & Firebase

Local edge database for offline operation and cloud synchronization for backup, ensuring data persistence and accessibility.

IoT Sensors

FC-28 soil moisture, JXBS-3001 NPK/EC sensors, pH-4502C, DHT22 environmental sensors with ESP32 microcontroller integration.

OpenCV & SHAP

Computer vision for image preprocessing and explainable AI for model interpretability, ensuring farmer trust and transparency.
